The Alignment Problem — Course B (early draft)

The second half of the pair: Course A builds the case for why aligning powerful AI is hard; Course B surveys the research programmes trying to solve it — the formal language of agents, the agent-foundations paradigm, concrete proposal families, and the frontier formal agendas — with each proposal judged against the obstructions Course A established.

This half is at a much earlier stage than Course A: the outline below is a working draft — readings, structure and framing will change as it gets the same treatment.

Capstone

Choose one approach family from this course. Map it onto two or three obstructions from Course A and write ~2000 words assessing how much of each it actually defuses, where it merely relocates the difficulty, and what would have to be true for it to work. Strong essays will cite the specific failure mode, not just gesture at it.
